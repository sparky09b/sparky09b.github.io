White Space and Clean Design

Ikea USA

White Space (or negative space) is the portion of a web page that remains "empty." It's the space between graphics, margins, space between columns, space between lines of type, or visuals.

Ikea uses white space to show where navigation buttons, search bar, wayfinding, and then their overall page of products.

Visual Hierarchy

Joann Fabric and Crafts

Visual Hierarchy is the order in which the human eye perceives what it sees. Certain parts of the website are more important than others, and you want those to get more attention.

Joann Fabrics and Crafts has the "One Day Fun Deals" call to action displayed first, and it is the attention-getter. Who doesn't want to have fun?

P.A.R.C. - Repetition

Disneyland Resort

Repetition is achieved by repeating an element more than once throughout a design. Proper repetition of content and elements on a page provides a sense of a cohesive relationship between those elements.

The Disneyland Resort site uses repetition in different forms. There is blue in the name, the image, and the navigation menus. There is white as the background, but also the image has white text. Who doesn't associate Magic with Disneyland?
